JAPAN GUIDE

Planning a trip can be overwhelming. Here, you will find the most popular attractions, foods, and shopping locations of each city and how to get around in one place!


Information from this webpage is sourced from https://www.japan-guide.com/
Tokyo
Part of the Kanto region, Tokyo is the capital of Japan and a popular tourist destination. Recognized for setting trends in fashion, art, and technology - it offers a blend of traditional and modern Japanese culture.
Osaka
Located in the Kansai region, Osaka is the second largest city after Tokyo. Known for its modern architecture and nightlife, it is the heart of Japan's cultural and economical development.
Kyoto
Once the capital of Japan, Kyoto is part of the Kansai region. Famous for its historical attractions, there are many temples, palaces, and traditional houses, Kyoto is where you can get a taste of rural Japan.
Fukuoka
Fukuoka is part of the Kyushu region in Japan. Known for its vibrant food scene, the city has been a center of international commerce since ancient times.
Nagoya
Located on the Pacific coast, Nagoya is the largest city on the Chubu region. Many of history's most famous samurai battles happened in and around the greater Nagoya region.
